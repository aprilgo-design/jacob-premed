What Each Program Actually Requires

Beyond grades — grade 12 courses, supplemental applications, videos, internal hurdles, and what they're really looking for.

What is a supplemental application?

Universities receive thousands of applicants who all have 90%+ averages. Grades alone can't tell schools apart. A supplemental application is a separate submission that reveals who you are as a person — how you think, what you value, and whether you'd thrive in that specific program's culture. A high GPA gets your application read. A strong supplemental application gets you in.

These applications reward genuine self-reflection, not impressive activity lists. A student who writes honestly about a moment of failure and what they learned will consistently outperform a student who just lists achievements. Jacob's thoughtful, introspective nature is a real asset here — the key is learning to translate that inner depth into writing.

Supplemental application details

Format: 2 written essay responses chosen from 6 prompts (2 questions × 3 options each)
Limit: 1,500 characters per response (~250 words) — strict limit
Deadline: Early to mid-February — window is only ~3 days once opened
What they assess: Critical thinking, authenticity, self-reflection, written clarity
Who reviews it: Faculty, staff, and Level IV BHSc students — scored by multiple reviewers
AI/help policy: Must be entirely Jacob's own work — consultants, AI tools, and discussing answers with others is prohibited and grounds for removal from the applicant pool
No CV or extras: No reference letters, resume, or extracurricular lists accepted — grades + supplemental only

📝 Example-style questions (past cycles):

"Describe a time you engaged deeply with learning beyond the surface — what drove you?" · "Design a supplemental question you would ask other BHSc applicants and explain why." · "Reflect on a belief you've changed and what caused you to reconsider it."

💡 Jacob's advantage: His thoughtful, inquiry-driven mindset fits McMaster BHSc's culture exactly. He should start keeping a reflective journal now — the best responses come from students who have genuinely reflected over time, not the week before the deadline.

📹 The video component — what this actually means for Jacob:

Jacob sees a question he's never seen before. He has 2 minutes to think. Then he records himself speaking for 2 minutes on camera — no editing, no retakes. This is intentionally designed to catch who people actually are, not a rehearsed performance. For an introverted student, this can feel daunting. The good news: Kira Talent allows unlimited practice with mock questions before the real thing. Jacob should practice speaking his thoughts aloud regularly starting in Grade 11.

💡 Example questions: "Describe a time you demonstrated resilience in a challenging situation." · "Tell us about an experience that shaped who you are today." · "What would you contribute to the Queen's Health Sciences community?"

⚠ The Hidden Hurdle — Internal Competition

Jacob applies and is admitted to Medical Sciences 1 — not the BMSc itself
Years 1 and 2 are called "Medical Sciences 1" and "Medical Sciences 2" — general science years with required courses
To progress from Year 1 → Year 2: must pass Medical Sciences 1000Y (non-credit) and score minimum 60% in each of Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Computer Science, and Math
Formal admission to the BMSc Honours Specializations (e.g. Pharmacology, Pathology, Medical Biophysics) happens after Year 3, based on a competitive weighted average at that point
This means Jacob's university GPA in first and second year is the real gate — not just getting in
No supplemental application required for entry — admission is grades-only from high school

⚠ Important reality check: The 15–20% acceptance rate the infographic shows is for Year 1 entry. Getting into the desired Honours Specialization in Year 4 is a second, separate competition based on your university marks. Students who coast in Year 1 and 2 can find themselves locked out of their intended specialization.

Financial Planning

Your family income: $156,000/yr. RESP: ~$15,639 total (Global + TD). Broken into semester payments.

OSAP Estimate — $156K household income

At a combined household income of $156,000, OSAP will provide loans only — no grants. Ontario's income thresholds for grants are roughly under $175K, but the grant portion effectively disappears well before that level for most families. Jacob will receive the loan component to help cover costs, which must be repaid after graduation (typically with a 6-month grace period before repayment begins). OSAP assesses both family income and the number of dependants. With one dependent in post-secondary, estimated annual OSAP loan is $5,800–$7,500/year. This reduces the out-of-pocket amount you pay each semester.

  • Apply through OSAP.ontario.ca every year before classes start
  • OSAP is paid directly to you, not the university — use it to cover fees due
  • Register for disability services early to access the BSWD grant on top of OSAP
  • OSAP loans are interest-free while Jacob is in school full-time
  • Repayment begins 6 months after graduation

Tips for Jacob

Practical, honest advice for the road ahead.

📚
Grade 11 & 12 are everything
Universities see your top 6 Grade 12 U/M courses. Consistency in Grades 11 and 12 signals to admissions that you can handle university-level work. Science tutoring this summer for Biology, Chemistry, and Physics will pay dividends.
🎯
Protect your GPA above all else
No matter which program you choose, GPA is the single most important factor for medical school. A 3.8+ is the target. This means being honest about your workload — a quad room at McMaster BHSc may actually serve you better than a "prestigious" program with a brutal curve.
📝
Start supplemental app prep early
McMaster BHSc and Queen's BHSc both require strong supplemental applications. These are about who you are beyond grades — your thinking, your growth, your goals. Keep a journal of meaningful experiences starting now. You'll need this material.
🏥
Get clinical exposure early
Volunteering at a hospital or clinic in Grade 11-12 matters. Not just for applications — it helps you confirm that medicine is actually what you want. Ask your school guidance counsellor about hospital volunteering programs in Toronto.
♿
Register for disability services immediately
Once your ADHD assessment is complete (CAMH referral is in), bring your documentation to every university's accessibility office on day one. Extended test time and quiet rooms can meaningfully impact your GPA. Also apply for OSAP's disability bursary — up to $6,000/yr in non-repayable grants.
🧮
Fields Institute Math circles
You're already going on Saturdays — lean into this. It's not just good for math skills. It signals genuine intellectual engagement to admissions, and for programs like Mac iBioMed or Waterloo BME, it helps your application stand out meaningfully.
💰
Apply for every scholarship
Automatic scholarships happen without effort — maintain your average. But application scholarships like the Loran Award and Schulich Leader require effort starting in Grade 12. These are life-changing amounts ($70K–$100K) and worth the time investment even with low odds.
🎷
Saxophone and band are an asset
Don't drop music. Medical schools value well-rounded applicants. A long-term commitment to an instrument shows discipline, creativity, and the kind of sustained effort that predicts success in medicine. It's also a genuine differentiator in supplemental applications.
🏃
This is a marathon, not a sprint
The path to medicine takes 11–17 years from now. There will be hard years. The students who make it are not the ones who burned brightest in Grade 12 — they're the ones who were consistent, took care of their mental health, and asked for help when they needed it.
